What is Phishing?

Phishing is a type of social engineering attack that uses emails, social media, and phone communications to deceive targets into providing their sensitive information. Attackers can achieve this by posing as legitimate organisations or individuals to gain the trust of targets.

What are the indicators of phishing?

There are many indicators of phishing attacks, the main indicators can include:

  • The sender's email address doesn't match an individual or organisation's email. You should always check the sender email for any differences.
  • The email subject or content creates a sense of urgency or fear. Such as: Critical Alert! Someone has logged into your account!
  • The email is requesting sensitive / personal information from you, this is very suspicious behaviour.
  • Grammar errors within the email's content. Especially when the email is sent by an organisation.
  • The email has suspicious links. You should always hover over links and check the destination before clicking on them. Furthermore, links that are hidden behind URL shorteners such as tinyurl.com should be checked.
  • The email has suspicious attachments. You should always check the attachment file type, for example, if it is an executable file such an .exe, it is likely malicious.
